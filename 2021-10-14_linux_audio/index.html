OSS

Open Sound System

OSS

  • Used until Linux 2.5
  • Uniform Kernel API for both apps and device drivers
  • Only one audio app could play at a time
  • Pretty much dead now (sorry, die hard fans)

ALSA

Advanced Linux Sound Architecture

ALSA

  • Replaces OSS since Linux 2.6
  • Multiple apps and sound cards can be mixed together
  • Better USB support
  • Bluetooth support
  • Midi devices
  • And more...

PulseAudio

PulseAudio

  • An audio server on top of ALSA
  • Much less configuration needed than ALSA
  • Cross platform compatibility
    (BSD, Linux, Solaris, MacOS, etc.)
  • Easy to play multiple apps though multiple sound cards
  • Hot plug USB sound cards

JACK

JACK Audio Connection Kit

Pulse and JACK are not competing

  • Pulse is for day to day use
  • JACK is for professional audio production

JACK

  • An audio server on top of ALSA
  • Uses only one sound card
  • Low latency
  • Full control of audio routing
  • For use with DAWs, drum machines, etc.
  • Make apps run perfectly in sync with one another

PipeWire

PipeWire

  • Brand new
  • Replaces and in compatible with both Pulse and JACK
  • Use multiple sound cards at once
  • Low latency
  • Full control over audio routing like JACK
  • Simple to use like Pulse (mostly)

PipeWire

It's been amazing for my online guitar lessons

PipeWire
